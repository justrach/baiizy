What Baiizy is: a social concierge for the moment when you'd like to see a friend but don't know how to propose it without making it weird. You tell it what kind of hang you want — deep work, late supper, low-pressure date, friend walk — and it answers with three real places nearby, a grounded reason each one fits, a one-sentence opener you could paste into a text ("coffee at 10, leave by noon"), and the distance from every friend who might come along. Target user: someone with four friends they'd love to grab lunch with but never pulls it together because picking a venue, matching availability, and proposing plans are all friction. Baiizy collapses all three into one tap.

Where GrabMaps powers it: every place comes from Grab. Onboarding neighborhood picker autocompletes against /maps/poi/v1/search with pinned coords. Event venue picker, same endpoint. AI agent calls /maps/poi/v1/search with intent-derived keywords biased by live location. Map pages render Grab's own urban-light-partner style from /api/style.json with Bearer auth, tiles from /api/maps/tiles/v2/vector/karta-v3 and internal-poi-v3, sprite from /styles/urban-light/sprite, glyphs from /fonts/{fontstack}/{range}.pbf. Every request proxies through our authenticated server-side route so the Bearer never touches the client. A /map3 page renders the full 123-layer style 1:1 as a demo. The "Tap in" spontaneous event creator finds the nearest cafe to the midpoint between you and a nearby friend via Grab and invites them in one click.

Picks: a freeform search bar takes any phrase as the Grab keyword, or tap an intent pill. GPT-5.4-nano returns three grounded picks in ~4s via Vercel AI Gateway, output constrained with Zod, 30s abort, fallbacks to Claude Haiku 4.5 and Gemini 2.5 Flash.

Free Together and the group algorithm: a dark panel above the picks computes which availability slots you share with accepted friends, shows stacked avatars of who'd be in, and offers a one-tap "show picks" filtered to that slot. Tap a slot and the recommendations endpoint pulls the live location of every friend who's free then, computes the centroid of the whole group, and runs Grab search from that midpoint. Each card displays per-friend distance chips — "you 0.9km, Maya 1.2km, Priya 1.5km, max 1.5km" — so the optimization is visible and auditable.

Location correctness: Grab POI search is relevance-ranked, not distance-ranked — a "lunch" search near Holland Village returned Jurong Point fifteen kilometres away. We cap candidates at 3km haversine before the LLM, fall back to the two closest within 10km only if empty, and surface which cached location drove each result.

Friends Map: Zenly-style. Circular avatar inside colored ring anchored dead-center on the real coordinate, 2.4s pulse animation, hover scale. Tap any marker and the camera fly-to at zoom 16 with 24° pitch. fitBounds caps at 13.5 for the group frame. Keyboard works after focusing the canvas — arrows pan, +/- zoom, shift-arrows rotate.

Graceful degradation: Grab's style, tile, and font endpoints each 502'd at different points during the hackathon — 15 distinct issues in bugs/grab-api.md. We snapshotted Grab's vector style while live and route every asset through our proxy with three-attempt exponential backoff. When the font service 5xxs, the proxy substitutes Protomaps' Noto Sans glyphs with an x-baiizy-font-fallback header. When the tile CDN is fully unreachable, we drop to a warm-tinted Carto Voyager raster style and show an amber "Not GrabMaps" pill.

The rest: check-ins fan out notifications to every accepted friend via a bell in the /users header. Profile photos upload to a private Cloudflare R2 bucket and serve through our authenticated proxy. Events are Luma-style with Grab-powered venue search, invites, RSVPs, five-star reviews averaged per venue. Onboarding stores a pgvector embedding for similarity-ranked friend matching.

Stack: Next.js 16 with Turbopack, React 19, Drizzle on PlanetScale Postgres with pgvector HNSW, better-auth, Cloudflare R2, Vercel AI Gateway, MapLibre GL, GrabMaps everywhere geographic.

What to notice is the shape. Every recommendation ends with a message you could actually send. Every group query shows its work. Every upstream outage is handled without the user seeing a broken page. The Free Together overlap, the centroid search, the per-friend distance chips, the avatar-centered markers at zoom 16 — one coherent answer to one question: can we make going out with friends less heavy?
