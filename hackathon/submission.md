Baiizy is a social concierge for the moment when you'd like to see a friend but don't know how to propose it without making it weird. You tell it what kind of hang you want — deep work, late supper, low-pressure date, friend walk — and it answers with three real places nearby, a grounded reason each one fits, a one-sentence opener you could paste into a text ("coffee at 10, leave by noon"), and the distance from every friend who might come along.

Where GrabMaps powers the product: every place comes from Grab. The onboarding neighborhood picker autocompletes against /maps/poi/v1/search and pins exact coordinates. The event venue picker uses the same endpoint. The AI agent calls /maps/poi/v1/search with intent-derived keywords biased by the user's live location. Our map pages render GrabMaps' own urban-light-partner style from /api/style.json with Bearer auth, pulling vector tiles from /api/maps/tiles/v2/vector/karta-v3 and internal-poi-v3, sprite from /styles/urban-light/sprite, and glyphs from /fonts/{fontstack}/{range}.pbf. Every request goes through our authenticated server-side proxy so the Bearer token never touches the client. A dedicated /map3 page renders the full 123-layer Grab style end-to-end as a 1:1 demo. The spontaneous "Tap in" event creator finds the nearest cafe to the midpoint between you and a nearby friend via Grab and invites them in one click.

The Picks tab is where most of the product lives. A freeform search bar takes any phrase and uses it as the Grab keyword. Or tap an intent pill and let the agent pick keywords. Either way, GPT-5.4-nano returns three grounded picks in about four seconds via Vercel AI Gateway — output constrained to three with a Zod schema, a 30-second abort on the request, and fallbacks to Claude Haiku 4.5 and Gemini 2.5 Flash.

Above the picks sits a dark Free Together panel. For every availability slot we compute which of your friends are also free, show stacked avatars of who'd be in, and offer a one-tap "show picks" filtered to that slot's natural intent. Tap a slot and the recommendations endpoint pulls the live location of every friend who's free then, computes the centroid of the whole group, and runs the Grab search from that midpoint. Each card shows per-friend distance chips: "you 0.9km · Maya 1.2km · Priya 1.5km · max 1.5km". The group optimization is visible. You can audit the math.

Grab's POI search is relevance-ranked, not distance-ranked — a "lunch" search near Holland Village returned Jurong Point fifteen kilometres away. We cap candidates at 3km haversine before the LLM, fall back to the two closest within 10km only if empty, and surface which location drove the result.

The Friends Map is Zenly-style. Each friend is a circular avatar inside a colored ring, anchored dead-center on their real coord with a 2.4-second pulse animation. Tap any marker and the camera does a 900ms fly-to at zoom 16 with 24° pitch. fitBounds caps at 13.5 when framing the whole group. Keyboard works after a click to focus the canvas — arrows pan, +/- zoom, shift-arrows rotate, with an amber outline showing focus.

Graceful degradation is the story we're proudest of. Grab's style, tile, and font endpoints each returned 502s at different points during the hackathon — fifteen distinct issues documented in bugs/grab-api.md. We snapshotted Grab's vector style while live and route every asset through our proxy with three-attempt exponential backoff. When the font service 5xxs, the proxy transparently substitutes Protomaps' Noto Sans glyphs with an x-baiizy-font-fallback header so labels keep rendering. When the tile CDN is fully unreachable, we drop to a warm-tinted Carto Voyager raster style and show an amber "Not GrabMaps" pill.

Profile photos upload to a private Cloudflare R2 bucket and serve through our own proxy. A nearby-friend banner pulses amber when any friend is within 1km. Events are Luma-style with Grab-powered venue search, invites, RSVPs, and five-star reviews.

Stack: Next.js 16 with Turbopack, React 19, Drizzle on PlanetScale Postgres with pgvector, better-auth, Cloudflare R2, Vercel AI Gateway, MapLibre GL, GrabMaps everywhere geographic.

What to notice is the shape. Every recommendation ends with a message you could actually send. Every group query shows its work. Every upstream outage is handled without the user seeing a broken page. The Free Together overlap, the centroid search, the per-friend distance chips, the avatar-centered markers at zoom 16 — they're a coherent answer to one question: can we make going out with friends less heavy?
