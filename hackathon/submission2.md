Most people have four friends they'd love to grab lunch with and never actually do, because picking a venue is friction, matching availability is friction, and proposing plans is friction. Baiizy is a social concierge that collapses all three into one tap. You tell it what hang you're in the mood for — deep work, late supper, low-pressure date, friend walk — and it returns three real places nearby, a grounded reason each one fits, a one-sentence opener you could paste into a text ("coffee at 10, leave by noon"), and the exact distance from every friend who might come along. The end state is a message you could actually send, not another places API wrapper.

GrabMaps powers every place in the product. The onboarding neighborhood picker autocompletes against /maps/poi/v1/search and pins exact coordinates. Event venue picker, same endpoint. The AI agent calls /maps/poi/v1/search with intent-derived keywords biased by live location. Map pages render Grab's own urban-light-partner style from /api/style.json with Bearer auth, tiles from /api/maps/tiles/v2/vector/karta-v3 and internal-poi-v3, sprite from /styles/urban-light/sprite, glyphs from /fonts/{fontstack}/{range}.pbf. Every request proxies through our authenticated server-side route so the Bearer never touches the client. The /maps3 page renders the full 123-layer Grab vector style 1:1 — open it and you're looking at Grab's production cartography streaming through our proxy.

Picks tab: a freeform search bar takes any phrase as the Grab keyword, or tap an intent pill. GPT-5.4-nano returns three grounded picks in about four seconds via Vercel AI Gateway, output constrained with a Zod schema, thirty-second abort, fallbacks to Claude Haiku 4.5 and Gemini 2.5 Flash. Above it sits Free Together, a panel that computes which availability slots you share with accepted friends and shows stacked avatars of who'd be in. Tap a slot and the recommendations endpoint pulls the live location of every friend who's free then, computes the centroid of the whole group, and runs the Grab search from that midpoint. Each card displays per-friend distance chips — "you 0.9km, Maya 1.2km, Priya 1.5km, max 1.5km" — so the group optimization is visible and auditable.

Location correctness took real work. Grab POI search is relevance-ranked, not distance-ranked — a "lunch" search near Holland Village returned Jurong Point fifteen kilometres away. We cap candidates at three kilometres haversine before the LLM, fall back to the two closest within ten kilometres only if empty, and surface which cached location drove each result.

The Friends Map is Zenly-style. Circular avatar inside a colored ring anchored dead-center on the real coordinate, 2.4-second pulse animation, hover scale. Tap any marker and the camera does a 900ms fly-to at zoom 16 with 24° pitch. fitBounds caps at 13.5 when framing the whole group. Keyboard works after clicking the canvas — arrows pan, +/- zoom, shift-arrows rotate.

Graceful degradation is the story we're proudest of. Grab's style, tile, and font endpoints each returned 502s at different points — fifteen issues in bugs/grab-api.md. We snapshotted Grab's vector style while live and route every asset through our proxy with three-attempt exponential backoff. When the font service 5xxs, the proxy substitutes Protomaps' Noto Sans glyphs with an x-baiizy-font-fallback header. When the tile CDN is fully unreachable, we drop to a warm-tinted Carto Voyager raster style and show an amber "Not GrabMaps" pill. The user never sees a broken page.

Rest of it: check-ins fan out notifications to every accepted friend via a bell in the /users header. Profile photos upload to a private Cloudflare R2 bucket and serve through our authenticated proxy. Events are Luma-style with Grab-powered venue search, invites, RSVPs, and averaged five-star reviews. Onboarding stores a pgvector embedding for similarity-ranked friend matching.

Stack: Next.js 16 with Turbopack, React 19, Drizzle on PlanetScale Postgres with pgvector HNSW, better-auth, Cloudflare R2, Vercel AI Gateway, MapLibre GL, GrabMaps everywhere geographic.

The shared PlanetScale Postgres has a tight connection cap and is flaky under judging traffic, so the authenticated flow at /login may 500 — prefer the video and /maps3 above. One answer to one question: can we make going out with friends less heavy?
